Database Comparison Tool

Manage databases on the go

Overview

Focusing on the microinteractions, I designed a database comparison tool which allows users to more easily manage databases.

Tools Used

Paper Protyping, Gliffy, Keynote, Sketch, InVision



The Process


Research

As part of a class project, I was instructed to focus on a microinteraction of a task that is usually done on a desktop but that could be optimized to also use on a mobile phone. After researching several people to gather more information about the kinds of tasks that they performed on desktops but were unable to or was not a good experience to perform. The three tasks I narrowed down were web development, database management, and video editing. I ultimately chose database management because it seemed to be the most useful.

Brainstorming

After narrowing down a few tasks that I could focus on, I began sketching possible solutions to the challenges I discovered from my research.

Concept Development

Before starting on this app idea, I did some competitor analysis to find any apps that have similar features as mine. Fortunately, I didn't find any. I proceeded to engage in a few user interviews to get a better idea of the audience I was designing for. I then pulled out some post-its and a marker to begin brainstorming features that I might include on the first iteration of design for the FitPik app.

User Testing

I tested my concepts using paper prototyping. Users were instructed to complete certain tasks to test the structure. Some of the feedback I received was that the column orientation was confusing, it was unclear that you could scroll or swipe for additional items, and that the tasks overall were fairly simple to accomplish. I decided to expand on the microinteractions of comparing databases.
