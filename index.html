Verbistro

Overview: Verbistro attempts to connect ESL teachers globally and allows them to buy and sell materials on an online marketplace. My goal was to redesign the web app to make it more user-friendly and appealing for users. The previous design was a bit more cluttered not as easy to navigate.

Role(s): UX Designer, Growth Hacker, Project Management

Tools Used: Adobe Photoshop, Affinity Designer, Mockingbird, Magneto CMS

Research & Analysis

In order to effectively design for the audience, I obviously had to get to know them. I engaged in audience research where I did intensive online research on the market, competitors, and our audience to get a better understanding of my task. I then conducted user interviews where I was able to observe a user use our product and ask questions about their experience.

Design

Taking my ideas from pen and paper to Mockingbird, I created wireframes to lay out important components of the web app. From here, I was able to test out what worked and what didn't to provide a seamless user experience. After additional research and analysis of my wireframes, I decided that we should offer 3 different pricing tiers which easily show and compare the different plans.

Prototype

Testing and Implementation

I product managed an overseas developer team to quickly and accurately implement these updates. I'm currently testing out these changes and working on a third iteration of a redesign. So far I have found that the web app isn't as clear as it could be that it is a marketplace. The buy and sell CTA buttons lead to the most frequent visits to the product and pricing pages but I've found that there's not as much clicking on these as I'd thought. Stay tuned for updates and feel free to check out the web app below!

Conclusion

Challenges: Getting people more engaged with the product is the biggest challenge for this project. I have found that I'm able to easily increase traffic to the site but once there, they still aren't engaging with the product like I'd like. I'm currently testing different methods to increase engagement.

Overall, this was a very interesting project and I got the opportunity to work with an audience I haven't yet - educators. After additional feedback, research, and brainstorming, I would still like to make additional changes to the web app. We will soon be undergoing our third iteration of the design so I'm looking forward to implementing changes and continuing to improve the design.

Launch Next

Overview: The public transportation system in Detroit, MI is extremely unsafe and inefficient for many of the residents there that rely on it. A team of myself and 3 others had 36 hours to come up with a solution at MHacks, The University of Michigan's premiere hackathon. Our solution is a simple web app which shows high crime ridden areas on a heat map and routes the user around those areas. Whether walking, biking, or taking a bus, we wanted to make sure people were able to travel safer by being more aware of their surroundings. Rather than finding only the quickest route like Google Maps uses, we wanted to find the safest route.

Role(s): UI/UX and Graphic Designer

Tools Used: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Affinity Designer, Sketch, HTML/CSS, Mockingbird

Research & Analysis

I researched and collected online data to analyze. Here are a few of the findings:
- There were large areas on the heat map which did not show a lot of crime. These areas were near highways.
- Not all buses have security cameras on them.
- Bus schedules aren't reliable and commuters are sometimes late.

Design

Through sketches and wireframes, I began testing out various designs. I knew that I wanted to have something with a really large map and the navigation input fields either beside or above the map. Ultimately, I ended up deciding on a design with the route information on the left hand side and the map on the right hand side similar to Google Maps because it offers a familiar interface to the user and is very simple to use.


Prototype

Here I began trying out different visual elements of the app. I created the logo choosing the color blue to depict confidence and safety since we're dealing with public transportation, especially in a place where the public transporation system isn't usually very safe and therefore isn't use with confidence.

Conclusion

Challenges: Since this was done at a hackathon with time constraints and a lack of resources, I was unable to conduct all of the research and planning that I would have liked. Additionally, there were some issues with the development of the project so unfortunately, there isn't a fully functional web app that you can check out. I coded a mobile responsive protype which you can see here.

This was a really fun project to work on so I'm happy that I had the opportunity to design it. I see the future of TranSafe expanding beyond just showing the safest route and beyond the Detroit area. Weather conditions, showing buses that have cameras, a real-time feed with traffic updates, and other features are all additional ideas that we had for this app that we were unable to implement due to the time constraint.

RowdyHackers

Overview: As I was creating a hacker organization at the University of Texas - San Antonio, I designed and developed a website where people could come to learn more about us, view our upcoming events to see what we're up to and quickly sign up for events, contact us, and get their questions answered.

Role(s):UI/UX Designer & Front-End Developer

Tools Used: Affinity Photo, Bootstrap, HTML/CSS, JavaScript

Design

Unfortunately, I had to skip the research and analysis step because we just needed a site put together quckly. I wanted a simple one-page design to minimize the need for clicking through the site and display all of our important information on one simple page. I sketched up several ideas and ultimately decided on the one that is displayed. The big call to action as soon as you visit the site is our biggest event of the semester so we wanted interested students to be able to easily sign up with one click.

Prototype

Starting off with basic sketches of the design was a necessary step in this process. From there, I was able to get a better idea of how I wanted the layout and general "feel" of the web app to be. I experimented with various uses of our organization's colors while keeping a very clean and minimal look. I chose to use an image of mountains in the header to depict a sense of adventure and new territory. This is a brand new organization on campus focused on innovation so we wanted a simple photo to portray that.

Testing and Implementation

I developed this design using Bootstrap for a simple, mobile responsive design. We are doing some testing and analysis to figure out what is working well, we can improve on, and what we should add or change entirely. Since we skipped the resaerch and analysis phase in the beginning, we are now taking the time to go back and analyze our findings.

Conclusion

Challenges: The biggest challenge about this project was the fact that it was a solo project. Although I was able to start building up a team that I could receive feedback from and in which we could brainstorm together and bounce ideas back and forth, the initial design was developed on my own which made it harder to make significant improvements.

As we work toward restructuring our leadership team, we had to put a hold on wesite updates. We will continue testing and analyzing changes. Stay tuned for updates!

Overview: I assisted with designing the flow of Impact Health's referral program. I worked closely with our Growth Hacker and developers to figure out what we needed so that I could come up with a simple design. The general idea was that people would complete a survey and then share their unique referral code for a chance to win money. For every 3 people that signed up with us and qualified for health insurance, both the referrer and referee would receive $25.

Role(s): Designer

Tools Used: Affinity Designer and Keynote

Design

I took note of all of the requirements for this project and using some other sites who's UI/UX I admire for some ideas, I was able to come up with the design showed below. I ensured that the styling was consistent with the rest of our web app and branding to provide a seamless user experience.

Prototype

Testing and Implementation

After working with the developer team to ensure that my designs were implemented exactly as I had visualized, we finally launched the referral program. To handle the amount of traffic that was coming through, I created a script in Google Sheets which automatically calculated ansd showed who needed to be paid. That is, every referee and the referrer for every 3 people that they referred. The script highlighted payment recipients and color coded them based on referrers and referees.

Conclusion

Challenges: Working quickly and diligently to quickly come up with a design that was consistent with the rest of our product was the hardest challenge. I would've liked to spend more time on the design of the the referral program but with the fast-approaching deadline, I had to come up with something rather quickly.

As my first industry project to be launched for users, this was a very exciting project to work on. Designing the flow in addition to creating the script to help manage the referral program made this a very important learning experience. I now feel more comfortable with working at a startup under high pressure to deliver a product by a quick deadline.
