Griffith Observatory

Enhancing the Griffith Observatory experience

Overview

This is one of the projects I worked on for GD 62 (UX Design II) with a team of 4 other students. We were instructed to choose a highly visited place in the area to improve the user experience of. We chose Griffith Observatory and decided to create an AR app to enhance the experience of visitors. After deciding on our concept, we split the app up into different tasks which each of us focus on. My task was originally capturing and sharing but after further testing, we decided to instead focus on a navigation feature which I designed. Some of the main problems that we observed at the observatory were not enough interaction, a lack of lanugage support for tourists, and unclear paper maps which made navigation a bit more difficult than it should be. An mobile app that addresses these issues by incorporating an interactive way to experience the museum and sky through AR, has location tracking map which can route the user anywhere in the observatory, and an option to change the entire language of the app. We also included a few additional features to make the app more interactive including a game which allows users to catch floating objects in AR and also an audio feature which offers an audio tour and allows users to hear how they might sound on other planets.

User Flow






Card Sorting

We completed a card sorting exercise that allowed us to get feedback from our information architecture map. A group of about 20 other students each had a chance to try to restructure our information architecture map. This enabled us to get a better idea of how well the flow of our app was and what we could improve to make things more clear or make better sense. Overall, others were able to correctly group the cards fairly well. An important change we made after this activity was removing taking, sharing, and saving photos because they are self explanatory.
